## 4. Privacy & Safety

Apple's privacy copy avoids fear. It asserts control and calm confidence.

- "Your business is nobody else's."
- "Groundbreaking privacy protections."
- "Private Browsing in Safari locks browsing windows when they're not being used, blocks known trackers from loading on pages, and removes tracking added to URLs as you browse."
- "All messages sent with iMessage use end-to-end encryption between devices, so they're only seen by those you send them to."
- "When you make a purchase with Apple Pay, your card numbers are never stored on your device or on Apple servers."
- "Use [Optic ID] to authorize purchases and unlock passwords with the uniqueness of your iris. Optic ID data is private and encrypted, and it never leaves your device."
- "Data from cameras and sensors is processed at the system level, so individual apps don't need to see your surroundings."
- "Everyday reassurance." (Find My + Check In)
- "Out of range. Not out of reach." (Satellite connectivity)

## 5. Sustainability

Apple's environmental copy is specific and uses numbers — never just vague claims.

- "More recycled content? Naturally."
- "We're significantly expanding the use of key recycled metals in iPhone batteries, magnets, and circuit boards."
- "Our latest models contain 95 percent recycled lithium in the battery."
- "All iPhone packaging is made of paper that is 100 percent fiber based and can be easily recycled."
- "Apple Vision Pro contains 100% recycled aluminum in the frame and battery enclosure."
- "Our stores, offices, and data centers are already carbon neutral. By 2030, we aim to reach net zero for our entire carbon footprint."
- "Our disassembly robots — Daisy, Dave, and Taz — recover crucial materials like gold, cobalt, tungsten, and rare earth elements."
- "Designed with the earth in mind."
- "On track to carbon neutral."
- "Planet-worthy packaging."
